# Perceived Cost of Capital in Pakistan

Data extraction and variable construction pipeline for the empirical analysis of
the perceived cost of capital in Pakistan's non-financial corporate sector.

**Reference:** Gormsen & Huber (2024, 2025)  
**Data source:** State Bank of Pakistan — Financial Statements Analysis of Non-Financial Companies

## Pipeline overview

### Step 1 — Extract old panel (`01_extract_sector_panel_old.py`)

Reads the `2014-23` sheet from `2005-23.xlsx`.  Filters to 14 estimation-sector
aggregate rows (identified by 3-digit SBP codes 704–729).  Extracts 13 financial
statement items per sector per year.

**Output:** `01_raw_sector_panel_old.csv` — 150 rows (15 sectors × 10 years).

### Step 2 — Extract FY25 panel (`02_extract_sector_panel_fy25.py`)

Reads the `FSA_NFCs_FY25` sheet.  Identifies sector aggregates by matching
the Sectors column against the Organisation Name column.  Skips the 3 title rows.

**Output:** `02_raw_sector_panel_fy25.csv` — 90 rows (15 sectors × 6 years).

### Step 3 — Compute variables (`03_compute_variables.py`)

Constructs all research variables:

| Variable | Formula | Used in |
|---|---|---|
| `Inv_Rate` | (OFA_t − OFA_{t−1}) / TotalAssets_{t−1} | Models 1, 2, 3 |
| `CoC_Proxy` | InterestExpense_t / (D_t + E_t) | Models 1, 2, 3 |
| `P8_ROIC` | P8 KPI ÷ 100 | Model 2 (DV) |
| `Size_ln` | ln(TotalAssets) | All models |
| `Leverage_S1` | S1 KPI (pre-computed) | All models |
| `Cashflow` | Retention / TotalAssets | All models |
| `SalesGrowth` | (Sales_t − Sales_{t−1}) / Sales_{t−1} | Model 2 |
| `DepRate` | Depreciation / TotalAssets | Optional control |
| `CoC_Proxy_L1` | CoC_Proxy_{t−1} | Models 2, 3 |
| `CoC_Proxy_L2` | CoC_Proxy_{t−2} | Model 3 |
| `r_SBP` | **PLACEHOLDER** — SBP policy rate | Model 3 |
| `CPI_inflation` | **PLACEHOLDER** — annual CPI | Model 3 |
| `delta_r_SBP` | r_SBP_t − r_SBP_{t−1} | Model 3 |
| `Interaction` | CoC_Proxy × delta_r_SBP | Model 3 |

Winsorises `Inv_Rate` and `CoC_Proxy` at 1st/99th percentile over the full panel.

### Step 4 — Validate and export (`04_merge_validate.py`)

Computes percentage discrepancies for FY2020–FY2023 overlap between the two
panels, flags material differences (>5%), and exports model-ready CSVs.

## Sample sizes

| Panel | Total obs | Model 1/2 sample | Model 3 sample |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old (2014-23) | 140 | 126 (FY2015-2023) | see `in_model3_sample` flag |
| FY25 | 84 | 70 (FY2021-2025) | see `in_model3_sample` flag |

The two panels **must not be pooled** — their FY2020-FY2023 overlap values differ
materially (~9.6% for interest expense, ~8.1% for CoC proxy) due to different
firm composition.

## External variables needed before regression

Fill these placeholder columns in `05_model_ready_old.csv` and `06_model_ready_fy25.csv`:

- **`r_SBP`**: Annual average SBP policy rate, computed on a Jul–Jun fiscal year
  basis. Source: [SBP Monetary Policy — Policy Rate History](https://www.sbp.org.pk/m_policy/index.asp)
- **`CPI_inflation`**: Annual CPI inflation. Source: Pakistan Bureau of Statistics
  or IMF International Financial Statistics.

The `delta_r_SBP`, `Interaction`, and `Real_CoC` columns will auto-populate
on the next pipeline run once these are filled.

## Robustness specifications

The `robustness_excl` flag marks two sectors for optional exclusion:
- **710 Fuel and Energy** — regulated pricing distorts ROIC
- **729 Cement** — extraordinary capacity-cycle volatility
